Department of Wildlife Conservation

Protecting Sri Lanka's precious natural heritage

The Department of Wildlife Conservation (DWC) is the guardian of Sri Lanka's breathtaking biodiversity. Established in 1938, we dedicate ourselves to preserving the island's natural treasures through:

  • Safeguarding diverse ecosystems: Our network of protected areas, encompassing over 12% of Sri Lanka, provides vital havens for countless species, from majestic elephants to vibrant birds and unique reptiles.
  • Ensuring ecological balance: We enforce wildlife laws, preventing poaching and habitat destruction, and actively manage protected areas to ensure the ecosystem's health.
  • Empowering communities: We collaborate with local communities and organizations, promoting sustainable development and fostering a sense of shared responsibility for wildlife conservation.
  • Inspiring future generations: Through education programs and public awareness campaigns, we cultivate a deeper appreciation for Sri Lanka's natural wonders and inspire action for their protection.
